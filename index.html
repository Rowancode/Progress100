About Progress 100

Progress 100 is our community for inspiring overrepresented groups in tech to engage with diversity and inclusion challenges.

Countless research papers have uncovered the financial benefits of racial, ethnic, and gender diversity. Yet we're still to see significant improvements being made in tech.

Many organisations are doing phenomenal work to support and engage underrepresented groups, and this should not go unnoticed. But unless we all engage with the challenges that exist, we will always be able to say "progress could have been made faster".

It's time for us all to act as one, and go out on a limb to build a truly diverse, inclusive and meritocratic tech sector.

We know that change is hard. It's not always easy to even know where to start. But there are steps that can be taken that will get us closer to our goal. But we have to prioritise this. This cannot come second best to other objectives; it must be seen as a crucial part of building a startup.

Progress 100 exists to inspire and educate those of us who struggle with these challenges to build fundamentally better companies.

To begin with we are focusing on CEOs and management of early-stage tech startups, where we believe change can happen most quickly, and where we can have the biggest impact.

Making Progress

Getting started is the hard part. The first step to making progress is committing to make progress.

Progress 100 is building a community of like-minded founders wanting to change the world, and providing them with educational content, resources and tools that make it easier to build better, more diverse companies. It will take a concerted effort to reach a critical mass of people, both under and overrepresented, who are committed to making the necessary changes to succeed. But once that point comes, the world will be a healthier place.
